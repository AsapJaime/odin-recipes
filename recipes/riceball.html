Rice Ball

What are rice balls?

Japanese rice balls (Onigiri) are a staple of Japanese lunch boxes. They are usually shaped into rounds or triangles by hand and are a fun treat to make and eat!

Usually wrapped in nori (dried seaweed) or rolled in sesame seeds, consider shichimi togarashi as a topping. This is a Japanese spice blend made up of ground sesame seeds, orange peel, and chile pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 to 2 sheets dried nori seaweed (optional)
  • 4 cups steamed Japanese rice (sushi rice)
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • 1 ounce black sesame seeds (optional)

Steps

  1. Gather ingredients
  2. Cut each nori sheet into 8 or 9 strips and put about 1/2 cup of steams rice in a rice bowl
  3. Wet your hands with water to prevent rice from sticking
  4. Rub salt on wet hands
  5. Place steams rice in your hand and form into a triangle, making sure it is dense and thick
  6. Form rice into a round, a triangle, or a cylinder by pressing lightly with both palms
  7. Wrap rice ball with strip or two of nori, or sprinkle sesame seeds on them
